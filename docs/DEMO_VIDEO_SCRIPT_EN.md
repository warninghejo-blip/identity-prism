# Identity Prism Demo Voiceover - English

Target duration: 2:50-2:58.

## 0:00-0:13

Most Solana wallets look the same from the outside: an address, a balance, and a transaction history. Identity Prism turns that into a living reputation passport.

## 0:13-0:34

I open a wallet, and Prism builds an identity card: a total score, tier, trust grade, badges, rank, and a short dossier. In a few seconds, you can understand whether this is an active ecosystem participant, a new user, or a suspicious address.

## 0:34-0:57

The important part is that this is not a one-way on-chain judgment. Even if a wallet is weak or looks risky, the user can prove humanity through action: by playing, completing quests, improving trust recovery, and building engagement proof.

## 0:57-1:33

Sybil Hunt makes verification collaborative. Users scan suspicious addresses, review the verdict, confidence, and evidence, and can earn a bounty when the signal is strong enough. The database keeps growing with every hunt, improving future decisions instead of staying as a static score.

## 1:33-2:05

Then reputation becomes useful inside the product. In Prism League, identity turns into ship stats: speed, shield, firepower, and luck. These stats affect games, rewards, XP, achievements, and progression. The passport is not just a visual profile; it changes how the user moves through the product.

## 2:05-2:24

Coins close the loop. Users earn them through activity, spend them in the shop on frames, auras, skins, titles, and modules, and tournaments create competition, entry fees, prize pools, and reward sinks.

## 2:24-2:43

Around this core loop, Prism adds arena challenges, quests, leaderboards, notifications, Vault, and Black Hole. Payments are part of the economy layer, but they are not executed in this demo.

## 2:43-2:55

Identity Prism reads a wallet, explains it, challenges it, and gives users a way to build a better reputation over time. It is an on-chain identity layer that feels like a product, not just a report.
