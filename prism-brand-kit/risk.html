Risk Disclosure

Reusable disclosure for wallet-connected, token-aware, or transaction-signing Prism products.

Prism products may surface warnings, scores, or smart recommendations, but those outputs do not eliminate blockchain, wallet, or market risk.

1. Non-custodial risk

Users control their wallets. Loss of a seed phrase, approval of a malicious transaction, or interaction with a malicious dApp can lead to irreversible loss of digital assets.

2. On-chain finality

Blockchain transactions can be final and irreversible. Failed or malicious operations may not be reversible by the publisher.

3. Smart contract and infrastructure risk

Product functionality may depend on smart contracts, relays, RPC providers, indexers, wallets, mobile frameworks, or third-party services. Bugs or outages in these systems can affect service quality or result accuracy.

4. Market and token risk

Any PRISM-based purchase, reward, balance, or future tokenized experience may involve volatility, pricing changes, liquidity limits, regulatory uncertainty, tax consequences, and shifting redemption rules across products.

5. Vault and staking risk

Vault or staking participation may expose users to lockups, delayed withdrawals, changing reward mechanics, accounting failures, smart contract bugs, or future policy changes. Participation should never be framed as risk-free.

6. Phishing and impersonation

Users should verify domains, app sources, wallet prompts, contract addresses, and payment destinations. Shared brand surfaces make consistency important; mismatched branding itself can become a phishing vector.

7. Regional restrictions

Access to some features may be restricted based on jurisdiction, sanctions, or local regulation. Each Prism app should add its actual geo restrictions before launch.
